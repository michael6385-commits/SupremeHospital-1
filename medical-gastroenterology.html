Overview

Medical gastroenterology is a subspecialty of internal medicine that focuses on the diagnosis and treatment of disorders related to the digestive system.

Our Gastroenterologists are physicians who specialize in this field and are trained to manage a wide range of gastrointestinal (GI) conditions. These can affect various parts of the digestive tract, including the esophagus, stomach, small intestine, large intestine (colon), liver, gallbladder, pancreas, and rectum. Once a diagnosis is made, we develop treatment plans tailored to your specific clinical condition.

Precision Diagnostics

Tools for Optimal Gastro Health

Gastroenterologists use a variety of diagnostic tools including Endoscopy (such as upper endoscopy and colonoscopy), imaging (MRI, CT, Ultrasound), and specialized blood/stool tests.

Our specialists collaborate with radiologists, oncologists, and nutritionists to provide multidisciplinary care for patients with complex GI conditions.

Patient Guidance

Frequently Asked Questions

Gastroenterologists treat issues like GERD, peptic ulcers, IBD, IBS, Celiac disease, liver disease, pancreatitis, and colorectal cancer.

You should see a specialist if you experience persistent heartburn, severe abdominal pain, sudden changes in bowel habits, or need cancer screening.

Your first visit will involve a review of symptoms, medical history, and potentially scheduling diagnostic procedures like an endoscopy or lab tests.

Sudden changes should be evaluated promptly by a gastroenterologist to rule out clinical conditions and ensure your digestive health is on track.
