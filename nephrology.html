Frequently Asked Questions

While some types of acute kidney injury can be reversed, chronic kidney disease is typically managed to slow progression and maintain function through medication and lifestyle changes.

Your first visit will include a comprehensive review of your medical history, discussion of symptoms, and potentially blood or urine tests to assess current kidney function.

Referrals typically occur if your primary doctor notices signs of kidney dysfunction, chronic high blood pressure, or unusual results in renal-related lab tests.
