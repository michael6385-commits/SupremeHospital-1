Precision Assessment

Diagnostic & Treatment Tools

Pulmonologists use various diagnostic tools and procedures to assess respiratory health. These may include pulmonary function tests (PFTs), chest X-rays, CT scans, bronchoscopy, and sleep studies.

Depending on the condition, treatment may include medications, inhalers, oxygen therapy, pulmonary rehabilitation, lifestyle changes (such as smoking cessation), and in some cases, surgery.

"Early diagnosis and appropriate management can significantly improve the quality of life for individuals with respiratory problems."

Frequently Asked Questions

Pulmonologists treat a variety of respiratory issues, including asthma, COPD, pneumonia, pulmonary fibrosis, lung cancer, and sleep apnea.

During your first visit, the pulmonologist will review your medical history, discuss symptoms, and may perform initial diagnostic tests like PFTs or order imaging to assess lung function.

Pulmonary rehabilitation is a program of exercise and education designed to help people with chronic lung disease improve their daily lives and breathing capability.

Smoking causes progressive lung damage and is a primary cause of COPD. It is never too late to quit; stopping smoking can halt further damage and improve respiratory health significantly.
