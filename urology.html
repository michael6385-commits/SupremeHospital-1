Comprehensive Urology

Overview

Urology is the clinical and surgical specialty that emphasizes on the urinary tracts of males and females, and on the reproductive system of males. Medical experts having some expertise in the area of urology are known as urologists and are trained to detect, treat, and oversee patients with urological issues.

The organs covered by urology incorporate the kidneys, adrenal glands, ureters, urinary bladder, urethra, and the male reproductive organs (Testicles, epididymis, vas deferens, original vesicles, prostate and penis). Urology combines the management of medical (i.e., non-surgical) issues like urinary tract infections and benign prostatic hyperplasia, as well as surgeries.

Specialized Procedures

Technical Expertise & Treatments

Cystoscopy

Using a specialized telescope-like instrument to closely examine the bladder and urethra.

Ureteroscopy

Visualizing the inside of the ureters and kidneys using specialized high-resolution equipment.

Prostate Biopsy

Collecting small tissue samples from the prostate for precise cancer laboratory testing.

Nephrectomy

Surgical procedure performed to remove a kidney, often used as a treatment for cancer.

Vasectomy

Cutting tubes responsible for carrying sperm, providing a safe method of contraception.

When to see a Urologist

Common Symptoms to Monitor

In some cases, your regular doctor might have the option to treat mild urinary tract issues. Yet, if your symptoms are severe or don’t disappear, you’ll have to see a urology specialist.

  • Blood in your urine
  • Loss of bladder control
  • Pain when you pee
  • Pain in lower back, stomach, or groin (Kidney Stones)
  • Inconvenience having or keeping an erection
  • Enlarged prostate & Urological Cancers
